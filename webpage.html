Renzo Mendoza Rabanal


I am a BSc graduate in Mechatronics Engineering from the Pontifical Catholic University of Peru (PUCP), currently working as Research Engineer for the Brain-body Interaction Lab at Harvard University. My specialization lies in Control Theory, Dynamical Systems and Computational Neuroscience. I aspire to contribute to the research in developing mathematical or computational representations that describe the complex brain-body feedback system.

Robotic Platform for Medical Canes with Advanced Control

Renzo Mendoza†,  Ivan Yupanqui †, Alain Chupa, Macarena Vilca, Diego Arce.

Development of a modular robotic platform for walking canes to assist older people and people with disabilities. Design a robust switching controller for nonlinear multi-body systems using Linear Parameter Varying (LPV) modeling and Linear Matrix Inequality (LMI) approach to ensure system stability with  performance.

Underwater Image Acquisition System with Lighting Control

Renzo Mendoza,  Daniel Menacho, Dario Huanca, Diego Arce.

Designed and implemented an underwater system with inertial navigation and lighting control to register images to reconstruct deep-sea environments. Developed a lighting control strategy for low-light environment merging proportional-integral control and histogram equalization. Achived 60% reduction of energy consumption.
